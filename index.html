Cybersecurity and IT Risk Management in BAFI's and Insurance Company in Nepal

Comparative Regulatory Analysis of Cybersecurity, Artificial Intelligence, and Information Technology Guidelines

I. Information Technology: Foundational Infrastructure

The financial landscape of Nepal is undergoing a profound digital transformation. As the central regulator for the banking sector, Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) has established a robust hierarchy of guidelines. Simultaneously, the Nepal Insurance Authority (NIA) has introduced specialized directives emphasizing transparency and digital policy issuance.

Governance: NRB mandates Board-approved IT strategies with annual reviews. The strategic planning process is expected to cover both long-term and short-term horizons. In the insurance sector, transparency is key, with NIA requiring companies to publish vision, mission, and management profiles on their websites.

Outsourcing: NRB holds the Board ultimately accountable for outsourcing. NIA requires cloud data centers to be registered within Nepal to ensure data sovereignty and domestic accountability.

II. Cybersecurity and Cyber Resilience

Simple security is no longer enough. NRB's Cyber Resilience Guidelines 2023 represent a shift toward "Resilience"—the ability to anticipate, withstand, and rapidly recover. The framework establishes a strict 2-hour Recovery Time Objective (RTO) for resuming critical operations.

Five Pillars: The framework is built on Governance, Identification, Protection, Detection, and Response. Institutions must be able to complete settlement by the end of the day, even in extreme scenarios. NIA further mandates "Cyber Insurance" to minimize residual risks within the institution.

III. Artificial Intelligence: Ethical Governance

AI is the most recent frontier. The draft NRB AI Guidelines (Dec 2025) and National AI Policy 2082 prioritize citizen rights. Systems are classified as "High-Risk" or "Not High-Risk" depending on their potential financial harm or impact on fundamental rights.

Transparency: AI systems must be "explainable." AI-generated content must be clearly labeled, and customers must be informed whenever AI is used in decisions that affect them, ensuring the decision-making process is understandable to stakeholders.

Conclusion

Toward a Harmonized Resilience Ecosystem: The path forward requires a unified approach where IT Governance provides the infrastructure, Cybersecurity ensures resilience, and AI Governance establishes ethical parameters. The goal is a financial system that is secure, fair, transparent, and resilient.

Personal Resilience & Perspectives

Langtang Valley Trek

Traversing the 'Valley of Glaciers' was more than a trek; it was a meditation on recovery. After the earthquake, the valley's resilience mirrored my own professional journey. Standing at Kyanjin Ri, I realized that every uphill climb—whether in a career or on a mountain—requires a steady rhythm and a clear destination. This experience provides the mental clarity I use to solve complex financial puzzles.

Tsho Rolpa Glacier Trek

Navigating the route to one of Nepal's highest glacial lakes (4,580m) was a masterclass in risk management. The thin air and the sheer silence of the glacier taught me the importance of "Composure under Pressure"—a skill I apply every time I present a critical report to high-level directors. Nature doesn't negotiate; you must adapt.

Dhorpatan Hunting Reserve

Exploring the unique high-altitude plateaus of Dhorpatan allowed me to find perspective away from the ledger. The silence of the wilderness reinforced my belief that a leader must occasionally step away from the noise to find clarity. It is in these heights that I develop my most creative financial strategies and macro-visions.
